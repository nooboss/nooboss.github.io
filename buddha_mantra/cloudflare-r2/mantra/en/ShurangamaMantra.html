Shurangama Mantra

Teyata Om Anale Vishade Vira Vajra Dhare Bandha Bandhani Vajra Pani Phat Hum Trum Phat Soha

तद्यथा ॐ अनले विशदे वीरवज्रधरे बन्ध बन्धनि वज्रपाणिः फट् हूँ त्रूँ फट् स्वाहा

1. Meaning

This section of the Shurangama Mantra invokes Vajrapāṇi — the powerful Bodhisattva and Dharma Protector who subdues negative forces and safeguards practitioners on the spiritual path.

The mantra expresses the strength, purity, and fierce compassion of Vajrapāṇi, helping remove obstacles, dispel ignorance, and protect the purity of one’s mind.

It calls upon the indestructible Vajra power to guard against harmful influences and stabilize the practitioner’s meditation and wisdom.

2. Explanation of Each Phrase

- TEYATA
“Thus” — introducing a statement of truth.

- OM
A sacred syllable that purifies body, speech, and mind.

- ANALE VISHHADE
“Radiant and purifying fire” — symbolizing the burning away of defilements.

- VIRA VAJRA DHARE
“Heroic Vajra Holder” — praising Vajrapāṇi as the wielder of indestructible wisdom.

- BANDHA BANDHANI
“Bind and subdue” — restraining negative forces and obstructive energies.

- VAJRA PANI
“The Vajra Holder,” Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva.

- PHAT HUM TRUM PHAT
Powerful seed syllables that cut through obstacles and negative influences.

- SOHA
“May this be accomplished.”

3. Benefits of Recitation

  • Provides strong spiritual protection and dispels harmful energies.
  • Strengthens courage, determination, and stability on the path.
  • Purifies obstacles, ignorance, and inner negativity.
  • Supports deeper meditation by calming disturbances and distractions.
  • Invokes the blessings and protective power of Vajrapāṇi.
