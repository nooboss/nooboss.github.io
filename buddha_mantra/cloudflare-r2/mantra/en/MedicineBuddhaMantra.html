Medicine Buddha Mantra

Teyata Om Bekanze Bekanze Maha Bekanze Radza Samungate Soha

तद्यथा ॐ भैषज्ये भैषज्ये महाभैषज्ये राजासमुद्गते स्वाहा

1. Meaning

This is the sacred mantra of the Medicine Buddha, Bhaishajyaguru — the Buddha of Healing and Lapis Light. He made great vows to relieve suffering, remove obstacles, heal illnesses, and bring peace and well-being to all beings.

Reciting this mantra purifies body – speech – mind, supports healing of physical and emotional pain, and strengthens compassion and wisdom.

It also expresses the aspiration that the radiant healing light of the Medicine Buddha may illuminate us, purify our karma, and lift us beyond suffering.

2. Explanation of Each Syllable

- TEYATA
Means “thus,” or “thus it is,” inviting us to hear this truth.

- OM
A sacred seed sound that purifies body, speech, and mind.

- BEKANZE BEKANZE
“Relieve suffering, relieve suffering” — healing illnesses of both body and mind.

- MAHA BEKANZE
“Great healing” — healing at the deepest level of ignorance and karmic causes.

- RADZA SAMUNGATE
“O Great King, who has transcended all suffering” — praising the Medicine Buddha as the supreme healer.

- SOHA
“May this be accomplished,” sealing the aspiration.

3. Benefits of Recitation

  • Supports healing of physical illness
  • Reduces anxiety, sadness, and emotional pain
  • Purifies negative karma
  • Strengthens wisdom and positive energy
  • Brings peace, clarity, and mental well-being
