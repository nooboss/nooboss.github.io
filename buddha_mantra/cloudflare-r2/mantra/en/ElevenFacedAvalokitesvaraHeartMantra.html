20. Ili Mili Chitre Chite Jvalamapanaya.
21. Parama Suddha Sattva
22. Mahakarunikāya Svaha

1. Meaning

The Eleven-Faced Avalokiteśvara Heart Dharani is a sacred mantra associated with Avalokiteśvara (Guanyin), the Bodhisattva of Great Compassion. The “eleven faces” symbolize His ability to see the suffering of beings in all realms and respond to them with boundless compassion.

This dharani invokes protective, healing, and liberating energy. It is traditionally recited to remove fear, purify negative karma, and invite the compassionate presence of Avalokiteśvara into one’s heart.

The dharani reminds practitioners to cultivate compassion, patience, and loving-kindness, seeing and responding to the world with wisdom and a gentle heart.

2. Benefits of Recitation

  • Dispels fear, anxiety, and emotional suffering.
  • Purifies negative karma and dissolves obstacles.
  • Invokes the compassionate protection of Avalokiteśvara.
  • Brings inner peace, clarity, and healing energy.
  • Strengthens compassion, understanding, and patience.
  • Supports mindfulness and deep spiritual focus.
